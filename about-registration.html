Participant Registration

Long-term access (from assembly through disassembly) to the U.S. Department of Energy Solar Decathlon 2015 site is prohibited without proper registration.

Online registration is now closed. To register, please visit the Registration Tent onsite.

Please note: Each participant must register individually. There are no group registrations. Registrations are subject to verification.

Registration Types

Solar Decathlon registration categories (per the Solar Decathlon 2015 Rules) are described below. If you are not sure which category to use, contact the registration official.

  • Team Member

    An enrolled student, recent graduate, faculty member, or other person affiliated with a participating school and integrally involved with a team's project activities is a team member. Decathletes, faculty advisors, and team crew are all considered team members.

    Note: With the exception of team crew, all team members must be prepared to show verification (e.g., student IDs or documents indicating they are recent graduates) that they are eligible to be team members.

    Participating students may be considered Decathletes if eligibility criteria are met. Decathletes must be prepared to provide verification of eligibility. With this verification, you will receive a special Decathlete wristband (issued just prior to start of competition week).

    A team crew person is a team member who is involved with a team's project but who may be unaffiliated with a participating school; contractors, volunteers, team media, and sponsors are team crew examples. The Team Crew category is mainly intended for people who will be on site for a short period of time during assembly and disassembly.

  • Organizer

    An Organizer is a Department of Energy or National Renewable Energy Laboratory employee, subcontractor, juror, or observer working on the project. The Juror and Observer classifications are restricted to those invited by the Director or Competition Manager to fill those roles.

  • Staff

    Staff includes individuals working for the Organizers whose role is not described above. Examples of staff are Volunteer Coordination staff, Orange County Great Park staff, and others not directly under a subcontract with the National Renewable Energy Laboratory.

  • NREL Employee

    This registration type is only for employees of the National Renewable Energy Laboratory.

Event ID Photo Requirements

At onsite registration, you will receive an event ID that permits you access to the Solar Decathlon site. In most cases, the ID will include a photo, which should be submitted as part of online registration. These photos must:

  • Be color
  • Be taken within the last 6 months
  • Be taken in front of a plain white or off-white background
  • Be taken in full-face view directly facing the camera with no hats or sunglasses
  • Be in a square aspect ratio so that the height is equal to the width (Minimum acceptable dimensions are 600 x 600 pixels; maximum acceptable dimensions are 1200 x 1200 pixels.)
  • Be in JPG format
  • Not be selfies.

The Registration Official will reject your photo if it does not meet these standards.

If your photo has not been received by Sept. 7, you must bring an electronic passport-style photo with you (on a USB drive). No photos will be taken by registration staff. If you plan to pick up your event ID at the All-Team Meeting (Sept. 27), you must have completed your registration by Sept. 7. Because of the large number of event participants who pick up IDs at the All-Team Meeting, we may not be able to process your ID during this time if all the requirements have not been met (exception is the Decathlete Eligibility requirements, which are not due until closer to the start of competition week).

Helpful Hints

Past Solar Decathlon teams have found efficient ways to complete their registrations. Here are a few hints:

  • Put one person in charge of encouraging people to register.
  • Create a spreadsheet to track who has completed their online registration, including photos and Decathlete Eligibility documentation (if available before Sept. 7 and if applicable).
  • Contact the Registration Official to confirm the status of your team's registration.
  • Set up a photo shoot day when all team members have their photos taken. This gives a uniform look to all photos. Have team members wear their team uniforms.
